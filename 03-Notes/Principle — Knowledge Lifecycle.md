## Principle

A note moves through seven stages: Capture (dropped raw into `00-Inbox/`) → Process (worked through in a daily note) → Refine (rewritten as a proper atomic note in `03-Notes/`) → Connect (linked to a hub and at least one sibling) → Review (checked weekly for staleness or missed connections) → Reuse (drawn on by active work in `04-Projects/`) → Archive (retired to `99-Archive/` with `status: archived`, never deleted).

## Why

Left unprocessed, `00-Inbox/` becomes a graveyard rather than a funnel, and unlinked notes become invisible to both Gabriel and the Vault Brain — an unconnected note is functionally the same as a missing one. Naming each stage makes it obvious where a note currently sits and what the next step is, instead of leaving "finish processing this" as a vague intention. Archiving instead of deleting preserves history the way [[Document The Why]] preserves reasoning: a retired note might still explain a decision years later.
