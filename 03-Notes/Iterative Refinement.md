## Principle

Ship a rough version first, then refine it against real feedback, rather than trying to design the final version up front.

## Why

Upfront designs are guesses; the fastest way to find out what's wrong with a guess is to make it real and look at it. This applies as much to a video encode as to a vault architecture.

## Connects to

This is a core rule of [[Hub — Principles]], and it's the reasoning behind [[Big Model Designs, Small Model Executes]] — a big model iterates on the plan, then execution proceeds task-by-task so mistakes surface early rather than after everything is built.
