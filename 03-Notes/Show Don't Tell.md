## Principle

Demonstrate a result — a working encode, a rendered note graph, a running feature — rather than describing what it would look like.

## Why

Descriptions hide the gaps between intention and reality; a working artifact can't. It also compresses feedback cycles, which is the same instinct behind [[Iterative Refinement]].

## Connects to

Part of [[Hub — Principles]]. Where [[Fail Fast, Fail Cheap]] is about how quickly to find out something is wrong, this principle is about how you find out: by producing the real thing, not a description of it.
